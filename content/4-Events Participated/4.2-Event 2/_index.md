# “AWS VIETNAM CLOUD DAY 2025”

**Venue:** Bitexco Financial Tower  
**Date:** Thurday, 18 September 2025  

## Event Objectives
- Share AWS strategy, customer success stories, and leadership perspective on cloud & GenAI in Vietnam
- Provide executive-level insights on navigating the GenAI revolution and organizational change
- Equip practitioners with technical best practices for building data foundations and GenAI workloads on AWS
- Introduce modern approaches for AI-driven software delivery, GenAI security, and AI agent productivity

## Key Takeaways

### Design Mindset
- **Business-first approach:** Start from business goals and value, then map to technology choices
- **Executive alignment:** GenAI success depends on leadership sponsorship and organizational readiness
- **Change management:** Adoption requires cultural shift, new workflows, and governance—not only tools

### Technical Architecture
- **Unified data foundation:** Strong data ingestion + storage + processing + governance is essential for AI at scale
- **GenAI roadmap:** Use a phased approach—pilot → validate → productionize with guardrails
- **AI-DLC model:** Embed AI across planning, coding, testing, and maintenance with human oversight
- **Security by design:** Protect GenAI workloads end-to-end with encryption, access control, monitoring, and auditing
- **Agent orchestration:** AI agents need workflows, tool boundaries, and controls to be reliable in production

### Modernization Strategy
- **Phased approach:** No rushing—follow a clear roadmap from readiness to implementation
- **Operating model:** Define roles, ownership, and processes for GenAI delivery and operations
- **ROI measurement:** Measure business impact (cost reduction + agility + productivity), not just “AI adoption”

### Applying to Work
- **Assess readiness:** Review data foundation and governance maturity before scaling GenAI
- **Pilot GenAI use cases:** Start with high-impact, low-risk use cases and define success metrics
- **Adopt AI-DLC practices:** Introduce AI support in SDLC (code transformation, testing, documentation) gradually
- **Apply security fundamentals:** Implement least privilege, encryption, monitoring, and audit trails from day one
- **Prototype AI agents:** Try agents for triage/reporting/ops workflows with strict guardrails and observability


## Key Takeaways

### Design Mindset
- Executive leadership is critical: GenAI adoption is not only technical, but organizational and cultural
- Clear alignment with business objectives and change management determines success
- Treat GenAI as a multi-layer stack (data → model → app → security) rather than a single feature

### Technical Architecture
- A unified data foundation is a prerequisite for scalable AI/analytics success (ingestion, storage, processing, governance)
- GenAI roadmap requires platform thinking: choose services that support experimentation → production safely
- AI-DLC reframes SDLC: AI becomes a collaborator across planning, coding, testing, and maintenance
- Security must be designed end-to-end: encryption, access control, monitoring, and governance
- AI agents require orchestration and guardrails to scale reliably beyond prototypes

### Applying to Work
- Assess current data foundation and governance readiness before scaling AI initiatives
- Define a GenAI adoption roadmap with measurable outcomes and staged rollout
- Pilot AI-DLC workflows for selected projects (code generation, testing automation, operational tasks)
- Apply GenAI security best practices early (least privilege, encryption, monitoring, auditability)
- Prototype AI agents for high-impact workflows (triage, reporting, operational automation) with clear guardrails

## Event Experience
Attending “AWS Vietnam Cloud Day 2025” provided a complete view of GenAI adoption from both leadership and engineering perspectives. The morning keynotes and panel discussion emphasized strategy, culture, and transformation, while the breakout sessions delivered practical guidance on building data foundations, implementing AI-DLC, securing GenAI applications, and leveraging AI agents to multiply productivity. The combination of executive insights and hands-on technical direction made the event highly applicable for planning real-world GenAI initiatives on AWS.
